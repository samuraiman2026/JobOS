Workflow 02c · /referral
Referral-first strategy
Build the warm intro path before you apply. Referrals have 5× the callback rate of cold applications.
Referral strategy - how it works
  • 1
    Find the warm path first
    Before applying, identify who in your network works at or has worked at the target company. A warm intro 5× your callback rate.
  • 2
    Lead with value, not "I'm applying"
    DMs that ask for favors get ignored. Share a relevant insight, mention shared context, then ask for a 15-min call.
  • 3
    Apply + outreach in parallel
    Don't wait for a reply before applying. Do both at once. The referral increases visibility of your existing application.
  • 4
    Follow up once at day 5
    One short follow-up if no reply. Then move on. Track in your outreach table — never chase more than twice.

Workflow 06 · /negotiate
Offer negotiation
Market benchmarks, your leverage points, counter offer recommendation, and the exact call script.
Market benchmarks - how to research them
The /negotiate workflow searches current comp benchmarks for your role and market when you paste the prompt into Claude. You will get live data from Levels.fyi, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and compensation surveys.
To anchor your benchmarks before negotiating: search "[Role Title] salary [City] [Year]" on LinkedIn, Glassdoor, and Levels.fyi. Look at 10th/50th/90th percentile, not just the median.
Your leverage points (customize these)
The /negotiate prompt asks Claude to surface your specific leverage given the offer. Common leverage points:
  • Competing offers or active processes at other companies
  • Rare skills or credentials listed in the JD that you have
  • Specific metrics (revenue, growth, team size) that match their need exactly
  • Current employment status - consulting/advising = not desperate
  • Long ramp timeline at your current role makes switching costly
